Methodology: Both accounts invest $10,000 in the S&P 500 total return index. Roth IRA: no taxes on dividends, growth, or qualified withdrawals after age 59½. Taxable brokerage: dividends are taxed annually at 15% during working years, reducing reinvestment (tax drag). Upon selling, long-term capital gains are taxed at the selected withdrawal rate. Cost basis = original $10K + after-tax reinvested dividends. Dividend yield is approximated as a constant % of portfolio value (historical S&P 500 average ≈ 2%). No state taxes, fees, or inflation adjustment. This is a simplified model for illustration.
